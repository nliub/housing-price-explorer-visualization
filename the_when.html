Would interest rates affect the prices?

Let's examine mortgage rates too. Below is a series of connected scatterplots for the median sale price per square foot growth year-over-year plotted against the mortgage rates for the current month, from 3 months ago, and from 6 months ago.

The black circles emphasize the extreme inflation rates (either a maximum or a minimum).

In this case, thanks again to the lagging nature of home prices, we see a most pronounced negative correlation between mortage rates from 6 months ago and growth of median sale price (right most graph). Keep in mind that closing a house also takes a month or so. It appears that the housing market has some kind of momentum that delays responses to rising mortgage rates. It could also be that mortgage rates might not be the main driver of the market. Have some thoughts about the underlying market drivers? Share with us in the About page!
